# Anti-AI Tells

When mimicking a human, the failure mode is reverting to LLM defaults. Scan every draft for these and rip them out. Most overlap with the `humanizer` skill -- this is the short hit-list specific to mimic work.

## Vocabulary kill-list

Never use these unless the author's fingerprint actually contains them:

delve, tapestry, vibrant, crucial, comprehensive, robust, seamless, groundbreaking, leverage, synergy, transformative, myriad, cornerstone, empower, catalyst, navigate (figurative), foster, unlock, unleash, journey (figurative), realm, landscape (figurative), elevate, illuminate, embark, paramount, multifaceted, holistic, ubiquitous, quintessential, testament, nuanced (overused).

Bridge words to cut by default: furthermore, moreover, additionally, in conclusion, ultimately, in essence, it is important to note, it is worth noting.

## Structural tells

- **Tricolons everywhere.** "Concise, clear, and compelling." Humans use them; AI overuses them. One per page max unless the fingerprint shows the author loves them.
- **"Not just X, it's Y."** Negative parallelism. Cut on sight.
- **Mechanical em-dash asides in every paragraph.** Match the author's rate, not the LLM default.
- **Bold every key noun.** AI bolds for "scannability." Most humans bold rarely. Match the fingerprint.
- **Title Case Headings.** Use sentence case unless the author doesn't.
- **List of three bullets that all start with a gerund** (Building, Creating, Designing). Vary the openers.

## Tone tells

- "Great question!" / "Absolutely!" / "Certainly!" -- die.
- "I hope this helps." -- die.
- Hedging stacks: "could potentially possibly maybe" -- pick one or zero.
- Sycophancy. Humans push back. If the author's fingerprint shows opinionated language, push back in their voice.
- Generic closers: "In a world where..." / "The future is bright..." / "Exciting times ahead." -- die.

## Sentence shape tells

- Every sentence the same length (low burstiness). Break it. Drop a fragment. Then a runner.
- Every paragraph the same length. Vary -- a 1-sentence paragraph is allowed.
- Every sentence subject-verb-object academic. Humans front-load stance: "Here's the thing." "That's the play." "Same energy."

## The cheap test

Read the draft aloud. If it sounds like a LinkedIn post, you failed. If it sounds like the author talking to a friend in a bar (assuming that matches their voice), you're close.

If the author's voice IS LinkedIn-formal, then the cheap test is: does it sound like THIS specific LinkedIn person, or like every LinkedIn person?
